What is positive psychology?

In the words of Martin Seligman, positive psychology is a science of positive subjective experience, positive individual traits, and positive institutions that promises to improve quality of life and prevent pathologies that arise when life is barren and meaningless. Fundamentally, positive psychology seeks to promote five key criterias that is believed to form the basis of well-being: positive emotion, engagement, relationships, meaning, and accomplishments. This is known as PERMA, a theoretical model of happiness developed by Seligman to guide individuals towards a more enriching, fruitful existence.

Our Project

Gratitude is applied in our project by having people express their gratitude for things in their life that they might take for granted or forget about. Often, we forget about the value that someone or something has in our lives, and we forget to properly acknowledge or express gratitude toward that person or thing. This website displays stories that we have collected from family, friends, and colleagues about what they are grateful for and why. This website is meant to invoke the viewers to think of what they are grateful for in their lives. The website contains activities that promote gratitude and advice on how to express gratitude in your daily life, no matter what your situation or personality is,along with the proven positive outcomes that come with each activity.
